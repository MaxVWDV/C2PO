Greenland: Gonville Fjeld and Caius Fjeld

Gonville Fjeld and Caius Fjeld are located in the Stauning Alps, Greenland. These peaks were first climbed by a team from Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge in 1963. Gonville Fjeld is 1,994 m high and Caius Fjeld is 2,280 m high, with both having rugged, windswept rock summits surrounded by cliffs. The largely inaccessible peaks are located 50 km from the town of Ittoqqortoormiit in East Greenland, itself one of the most remote settlements on Earth.
